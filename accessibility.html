Auto-Paste (Accessibility) Guide

How to enable one-tap automatic paste from Melo's Clipboard history into another app.

Last updated: 2026-07-02

What is Auto-Paste?

By default, when you tap a clipboard history item in Melo, the content is copied to your clipboard and a brief toast tells you to press ⌘+V to paste it into the focused app. This works on every Mac without any extra permission.

If you want Melo to paste for you — no ⌘+V required — Melo needs the Accessibility permission. Because the Mac App Store requires us to keep the app sandbox-friendly, the App Store build does not register itself with macOS Accessibility automatically. You need to add Melo to the Accessibility list manually, following the steps below.

Why does Apple require this?

Accessibility is the only macOS permission that lets one app send keystrokes to another. Apple treats it as a sensitive capability so the user must approve it on a per-app basis in System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility.

Enable Accessibility for Melo manually

Important: The App Store version of Melo cannot open the Accessibility settings for you. Please follow every step below manually.
1. Open System Settings

Click the Apple menu () in the menu bar and choose System Settings.

2. Go to Privacy & Security → Accessibility

In the left sidebar, scroll down and select Privacy & Security, then click Accessibility on the right.

3. Unlock the pane

Click the lock icon in the bottom-left corner and enter your Mac password or use Touch ID.

4. Add Melo to the list

Click the + button below the app list. In the file picker, open your Applications folder, select Melo Suite (or the standalone Melo app you are using, such as Melo Clip), and click Open.

5. Turn on the switch

Back in the Accessibility list, make sure the switch next to Melo Suite is turned on.

6. Restart Melo

Fully quit Melo (⌘+Q) and open it again. macOS re-checks the Accessibility permission on every launch.

7. Try Auto-Paste

Open the Clip panel, click any clipboard history item, and Melo will paste it into the currently focused app automatically.

Troubleshooting

I added Melo but Auto-Paste still doesn't work

Make sure the switch next to Melo in System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility is really on. Then quit and re-launch Melo.

I see more than one Melo entry in the Accessibility list

This can happen if you have both Melo Suite and a single-feature app (for example, Melo Clip) installed. Keep only the app you are actively using enabled, or enable all Melo entries you use. Remove old duplicates to avoid confusion.

Melo isn't in the Applications folder

If you moved the app after installing it, use the + button in Accessibility to browse to wherever Melo is located. Auto-Paste will only work reliably when the enabled path matches the running app.

Can I use Melo without Accessibility?

Yes. Everything except Auto-Paste works without this permission. When you tap a clipboard history item, Melo still copies it to your clipboard and shows a toast; you can paste manually with ⌘+V.

Privacy

Melo uses Accessibility only to send the keystroke sequence that pastes your selected clipboard item into the app that is currently focused. Melo does not monitor other apps, does not read text you did not ask it to paste, and does not collect any accessibility data. See the Privacy Policy for the full data-handling details.
